A little more about me

I have always been passionate about new technologies and driven by a constant desire to learn. My main interests revolve around the automotive industry and motorsport, space exploration and creative fields such as drawing, 3D modeling, and prototyping...

Naturally, this passion led me to pursue studies in civil engineering at UCLouvain. I first earned my Bachelor’s degree in Engineering Sciences with a focus on mechanics and computer science. Later, I had to choose between these two areas that were equally important to me. In the end, I decided to specialize in mechanics, as computer science is easier to learn independently, while mechanics requires more structured study.

In September 2025, I completed my Master’s degree. My goal now is to keep learning and growing, this time alongside companies, by applying my skills and gaining real-world experience.
I am motivated, organized, versatile, and able to multitask… In short, I can do everything (except cook !)

• Multibody System Dynamics

  • This course covers the definition and classification of multibody systems (MBS) and introduces the main characteristics of computational tools for modelling and analysis. It explores formalisms for tree-like systems (e.g., serial robots) and closed-loop mechanisms (e.g., vehicles), including the definition of barycentric quantities and automatic generation of dynamical equations using Lagrange multipliers, the virtual power principle, and the Newton–Euler recursive algorithm. The course also addresses the coordinate partitioning method and numerical analysis techniques such as equilibrium, modal analysis, time simulation, and inverse dynamics. Practical applications include serial and parallel robots, road and railway vehicles, and multibody systems with flexible elements. Students complete a project involving the modelling and analysis of a multibody system or the review and synthesis of scientific publications.
